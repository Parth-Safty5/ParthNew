Warming Up Chit-Chat Questions

1. How do you react when your parents scold you?
When my parents scold me, I try to stay calm and listen to what they re saying. I remind myself that they care about me and want me to improve. Later, I reflect on their words and, if needed, talk to them respectfully to share my feelings.

2. How do you feel when someone insults you?
It hurts when someone insults me, but I try not to take it personally. I remind myself of my worth and avoid reacting in anger. Instead, I focus on staying composed and walking away from negativity.

ParthNew

3. How would you react when you are cheated by your friend?
If a friend cheats me, I feel disappointed and hurt. I would talk to them honestly about how I feel and try to understand their side. If they re truly sorry, I might forgive them but I d be more cautious in the future.

4. Why is it wrong to make a difference among friends who belong to a different class, colour or community?
It s wrong because every person deserves respect, regardless of their background. True friendship is built on kindness, trust, and understanding not on social status or appearance. Discrimination only creates division and pain.

(3) Why Do the Lines Begin with If You Can ?

The phrase If you can sets a conditional tone, encouraging readers to reflect on their behavior. It creates a sense of challenge and motivation, suggesting that mastering these qualities will lead to true maturity and success.

Poem Appreciation

  • Title: If
  • Poet: Rudyard Kipling
  • Rhyme Scheme: Regular rhyme scheme (abab)
  • Figure of Speech: Antithesis, Metaphor, Personification, Repetition
  • Theme / Central Idea: The poem offers timeless advice on how to live with integrity, patience, and resilience. It teaches us how to handle success and failure, stay humble, and make the most of every moment to become a true human being.

I stand before you today with a heart full of memories and a mind ready to step into a new world. As we bid farewell to our beloved school, I d like to share a few thoughts on how we can make school education in our state even better.

Education is the foundation of our future. But sometimes, it feels like we re all expected to fit into the same mold. So here are a few changes I believe could make a big difference:

First, we need more subject options that match each student s skills and interests. Not every child is meant to be a doctor or engineer some are born artists, athletes, or entrepreneurs. Let s give them the freedom to explore.

Second, our teaching methods should be more interactive and activity-based. Learning should feel like discovery, not just memorizing facts. Teachers should guide us like facilitators, helping us think, question, and grow.

Third, we must rethink exams. Instead of burdening students with marks, let s focus on assessments that build confidence and creativity. Education should empower, not pressure.
