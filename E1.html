1.1 Walk a Little Slower

Warming Up!
Chit-chat:
(a) Are you a talkative person?
"Honestly, it depends on who I’m with! Around my close friends, I can talk for hours, but I tend to be a bit more of a listener when I’m meeting new people."
(b) Do you like to share your thoughts?
"I do, especially when I feel like my ideas might help someone else or when I’m trying to figure something out. Sharing thoughts usually makes me feel more connected to people."
(c) What would you like to do after 10th?
"I’m really interested in exploring the science stream because I love understanding how things work, but I'm also considering taking up a creative course in graphic design."

4. Understanding Implications: In this poem, "walking" is a metaphor for navigating life. Here is what the specific lines imply:
(a) "Sometimes your steps are very fast…"
Implication: Occasionally, a parent's actions or decisions happen too quickly for a child to understand or learn from them.
(b) "Sometimes your steps are hard to see…"
Implication: Some life lessons are complex or subtle, making it difficult for the child to observe and imitate them correctly.
(c) "I would want to lead just right…"
Implication: The child hopes to grow up and provide the same clear, correct guidance to their own future children.
(d) "And know that I was true…"
Implication: The speaker wants to look back with the confidence that they lived with honesty and followed the right path, just as their father did.

7. Discussion Summary:
(a) Passing on ideas and traditions:
*Cultural knowledge is preserved by our ancestors and handed down through generations.
*These traditions survive through mythological literature, Vedas, folklores, and fables.
*Values are reinforced through the celebration of religious festivals and ceremonies.
*Communities often preserve these heritages out of a desire to maintain their unique identity.

(b) Following in the footsteps of forefathers:
*It is often beneficial to follow the path of our ancestors because of the proven moral values they provide.
*However, change is necessary when old traditions are based on superstitions that lack scientific reasoning.
*We must be open to modern ideas to ensure our moral standards evolve with the times.
8. Do you have a role model? What qualities do you appreciate in your role model?
My role model is Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. Known as the "Missile Man of India," I deeply admire his intelligence and his unwavering dedication to his country. Despite his high status as President, he remained a symbol of simplicity, humility, and hard work, always striving to inspire the youth.
9. Learning from Family Decisions:
I recall a time I wanted to ignore my family’s advice during a school trip to Lonavala. They warned me to stay away from deep water. Ignoring them, I tried to enter a river, slipped, and nearly drowned because I couldn't swim. I was saved by a teacher, but the experience taught me a vital lesson: my parents’ warnings came from a place of love and experience. I now realize they were right to be cautious.
