Q.1:

​(a) How are ideas, customs, and knowledge passed between generations?

​Ans:

1. Our traditions and core values are carefully preserved by our forefathers and handed down to us.

​2. We inherit cultural wisdom through mythological literature, the Vedas, and ancient folklores.

3. ​Moral lessons are often taught through fables and stories told by our elders.

​4. Religious ceremonies and festivals act as a bridge to keep our heritage alive for the new generation.

​5. Parents play a vital role in training their children to behave with grace and follow a disciplined way of life.

​

(b) Is it necessary for children to follow the footsteps of their forefathers?

Ans: Yes, it is often beneficial because it allows the younger generation to learn from the vast experience of their ancestors. By understanding the ways and styles of those before them, children can avoid making the same mistakes and carry forward valuable knowledge. However, it is also important to accept modern ideas and change old ways when necessary to maintain a high moral standard in a changing world.

​

Q.2 : Do you have a role model? What qualities do you appreciate in them?

Ans: My role model is Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the former President of India known as the "Missile Man". I deeply admire his brilliant intelligence, his unwavering dedication to his duty, and his incredible simplicity. His commitment to guiding and inspiring the youth of India is a quality I truly value.

 

Q.3. A time I realized my family was right:

Ans: My parents always had a strict rule that I could only watch television for one hour a day. As a child, I used to dislike this restriction, especially since my friends didn't have such rules at their homes. However, I now realize they were right. This discipline helped me maintain a healthy balance between my studies and leisure time. It taught me how to manage my time properly, a habit that still helps me today.
