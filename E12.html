English Workshop
1. Understanding the Poem
(a) How many stanzas are there in the poem?
There are a total of three stanzas in this poem.

(b) How many lines are there in the poem?
The poem consists of 12 lines in total (4 lines per stanza).

(c) List the rhyming words in each stanza.
o Stanza 1: light/night, free/sea.
o Stanza 2: call/all, drives/lives.
o Stanza 3: grove/love, glee/sea.
2. Rhyme Scheme
The rhyme scheme for every stanza in this poem is aabb.
3. Meaning of Expressions
(a) leaping wealth of the tide: This refers to the fish found in the sea, which are the source of income and food for the fishermen.

(b) kings of sea: This expression refers to the fishermen themselves, who master the waves and the ocean.

(c) at the fall of the sun: This phrase simply means sunset or the end of the day.

(d) the edge of the verge: This refers to the horizon, where the sea and sky appear to meet.

5. Time References in the Poem
(a) Early morning: "Rise, brothers, rise; the wakening skies pray to the morning light."

(b) Evening: "What though we toss at the fall of the sun where the hand of the sea-god drives?"

(c) Full moon light: "And sweet are the sands at the full o' the moon with the sound of the voices we love."
6. Courage of the Fishermen
The lines that show the fishermen are not afraid of the sea or drowning are:
"He who holds the storm by the hair, will hide in his breast our lives."
7. Landscape vs. Seascape (Stanza 3)
Landscape (The Land) Seascape(The Sea)
"Sweet is the shade of the coconut glade, and the scent of the mango grove." "But sweeter, O brothers, the kiss of the spray and the dance of the wild foam's glee."
"And sweet are the sands at the full o' the moon with the sound of the voices we love." "Row, brothers, row to the edge of the verge, where the low sky mates with the sea."

9. Nature Poem Translation
Original (Hindi): "प्रकृति की गोद में जो बसा है, सुख स्वर्ग सा वहां प्राप्त है। जहाँ नदियाँ कल-कल करती हैं, और पहाड़ खड़े शान से हैं।"

English Translation: "That which dwells in the lap of nature, finds a happiness like heaven there. Where the rivers flow with a gentle murmur, and the mountains stand with pride."
