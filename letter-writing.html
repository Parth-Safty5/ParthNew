Letter Writing Guide – Format, Examples, and Tips

Letter writing is an essential communication skill used in both personal and professional life. You can also improve communication through Report Writing. Understanding the correct format, tone, and structure helps you express ideas clearly and effectively.

What is Letter Writing?

Letter writing is the process of communicating messages through a structured written format. Letters may be formal or informal depending on the purpose and the relationship between sender and receiver.

Even in the digital age, letter writing remains important. Formal letters are widely used in job applications, official requests, complaints, and business communication.

A well-written letter maintains clarity, politeness, and proper organization. Using the correct format increases professionalism and ensures the message is understood properly.

Types of Letters

Letters are broadly divided into two main categories: Formal Letters and Informal Letters. The tone, structure, and purpose differ depending on the type.

1. Formal Letters

Formal letters are written for official or professional purposes. For structured academic writing, you can also learn Essay Writing Format. These letters follow a specific format and maintain a polite, respectful, and professional tone.

Formal letters are commonly used for job applications, business communication, complaints, official requests, and academic submissions.

  • ✔ Job Application Letter
  • ✔ Complaint Letter
  • ✔ Business Letter
  • ✔ Official Request Letter
  • ✔ Leave Application

2. Informal Letters

Informal letters are written to friends, family members, or relatives. You can also practice storytelling skills through Story Writing. These letters use a friendly and personal tone. The structure is flexible compared to formal letters.

  • ✔ Letter to a Friend
  • ✔ Invitation Letter
  • ✔ Thank You Letter
  • ✔ Congratulation Letter

Structure of a Formal Letter

A formal letter follows a clear and organized format. Maintaining proper structure increases professionalism and improves clarity.

1. Sender’s Address

Write the sender’s address at the top left corner. This helps the recipient identify where the letter is coming from.

2. Date

Mention the date below the sender’s address. Use a clear and standard date format.

3. Receiver’s Address

Include the designation and address of the recipient. This is important in official communication.

4. Subject Line

The subject briefly states the purpose of the letter. It should be concise and directly related to the topic.

5. Salutation

Use respectful greetings such as “Dear Sir/Madam” or the recipient’s name with proper title.

6. Body of the Letter

The body contains the main message. It should be divided into clear paragraphs:

  • ✔ Introduction paragraph
  • ✔ Main content paragraph
  • ✔ Closing paragraph

7. Closing & Signature

End the letter with polite closing words such as “Yours sincerely” or “Yours faithfully” followed by your signature and name.

Structure of an Informal Letter

Informal letters are more flexible compared to formal letters. They are written to friends, relatives, or close acquaintances. The tone is friendly, warm, and conversational.

1. Sender’s Address

Write your address at the top left corner. This is optional in personal letters but generally recommended.

2. Date

Mention the date below your address.

3. Greeting

Use friendly greetings such as “Dear Rahul,” or “My dear Friend.”

4. Body

The body of an informal letter can be divided into:

  • ✔ Opening paragraph (asking about well-being)
  • ✔ Main content paragraph
  • ✔ Closing paragraph

5. Closing

End with friendly closing phrases such as “Yours lovingly,” “Best wishes,” or “Take care.”

Example of a Formal Letter

Subject: Application for Leave

From,
Rahul Sharma
45 Green Park Road
Nagpur

Date: 5 March 2026

To,
The Principal
ABC Public School
Nagpur

Dear Sir,

I respectfully request leave for three days due to medical reasons. I am currently unwell and have been advised rest by the doctor.

I kindly request you to grant me leave from 6 March to 8 March 2026. I assure you that I will complete all pending work after returning.

Thanking you.

Yours sincerely,
Rahul Sharma

This example shows how a formal letter maintains clarity, politeness, and proper structure.

Example of an Informal Letter

21 Civil Lines
Nagpur

Date: 5 March 2026

Dear Amit,

I hope you are doing well. It has been a long time since we last met. I wanted to share some exciting news with you.

Last week, our school organized an annual function. I participated in a debate competition and won first prize. The experience was wonderful, and I learned a lot from it.

I hope we can meet during the upcoming holidays. Please convey my regards to your parents.

Yours lovingly,
Rahul

This example demonstrates the friendly tone and flexible structure used in informal letters.

Important Letter Writing Tips

  • ✔ Always understand the purpose before writing.
  • ✔ Use simple and clear language.
  • ✔ Maintain proper format for formal letters.
  • ✔ Avoid grammatical and spelling errors.
  • ✔ Keep paragraphs short and organized.
  • ✔ Use polite expressions in official communication.

Planning your letter before writing improves clarity. Always revise your letter to correct mistakes and improve sentence structure.

Common Mistakes in Letter Writing

  • ✖ Incorrect format in formal letters
  • ✖ Using informal language in official letters
  • ✖ Writing very long paragraphs
  • ✖ Missing subject line in formal letters
  • ✖ Spelling and grammar mistakes

Avoiding these common errors helps in writing effective, professional, and impactful letters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the main difference between formal and informal letters?

Formal letters follow a fixed structure and professional tone, while informal letters are personal and flexible in style.

2. Is the subject line necessary in informal letters?

No, subject lines are generally used only in formal letters.

3. How long should a letter be?

The length depends on the purpose. A letter should be clear, concise, and directly related to the topic.

4. Can I use contractions in formal letters?

It is better to avoid contractions in formal letters. Use complete words to maintain professionalism.
