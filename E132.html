3.2 The Fall of Troy - English Workshop and Warming Up Solutions

English Workshop
1. Find and write the Greek and Trojan names used in the story (Part I and II).
Greeks Trojans
Helen, Menelaus, Agamemnon, Achilles, Odysseus, Diomedes, Nestor Paris, King Priam, Hector, Aeneas, Cassandra, Laocoon
2. List all the words related to ‘war’ from both parts of the story.
Enemy, attack, siege, fortress, battle, soldiers, heroes, arrows, spears, fleet, army, victory, defeat, surrender, armor, fighting, invasion.
3. Note the following constructions carefully and then use them to express your ideas:
(a) A horse big enough to hold men inside it.
Ans: The mangoes are sweet enough to eat without sugar .

(b) The Greeks were tired of the long war.
Ans: She was tired of waiting for the bus.

(c) They are afraid of the long voyage home, too.
Ans: Many children are afraid of the dark.

(d) They made it too big to go inside your city.
Ans: The box is too heavy to lift alone.

(e) They were so excited that they paid no attention to his words.
Ans: He was so tired that he fell asleep instantly.
4. Put the following events in the order in which they took place. Number them accordingly.
(c) The cunning Odysseus thought of a plan. [1]
(f) The Greeks built a big wooden horse. [2]
(g) The great heroes hid inside the horse. [3]
(d) The Greeks burnt their tents and sailed away. [4]
(i) The Trojans were happy to see the Greek ships go. [5]
(a) The Trojans found a Greek man under the big wooden horse. [6]
(h) The priest warned the Trojans not to break the wall. [7]
(b) They broke down part of the horse and brought the horse in. [8]
(j) The Trojans slept soundly. [9]
(k) The Greeks came out of the horse and opened the gates. [10]
(l) The Greek army entered the city. [11]
(e) Troy was burnt down. [12]
5. Form pairs. Imagine you are a pair of Trojans and you have come to know about Odysseus’s plan. Make a counter plan to defeat the Greeks.
Our Counter Plan:

"Fellow Trojans, the horse is a trap! We have discovered that Odysseus and his men are hiding inside it. Instead of bringing it into our city, we must surround it quietly at night. We will drill small holes in the bottom and smoke them out using burning sulfur. As the Greeks flee the horse, we will capture them one by one. Meanwhile, we must keep our torches ready on the walls; if the Greek fleet tries to return under the cover of darkness, we will set their ships on fire before they even reach the shore!"
6. (a) Refer to the Language Study pages and read the following entries :
(i) word (ii) phrase (iii) clause (iv) sentence.
6. (b) Write three phrases on your own.
A phrase is a group of words that makes sense but not complete sense.

• Over the high mountains.
• A chest full of gold.
• Running through the fields.
Write three sentences using your own ideas.
A sentence expresses a complete idea and must have a subject, a predicate, and at least one finite verb.

• The brave soldiers defended the city gates.
• Birds fly south for the winter.
• She wrote a long letter to her friend.
(c) Identify one example of a main clause and one example of a dependent clause
Main Clause: "The man pretended to be frightened of them".
(This expresses a complete thought and can stand alone as a sentence.)

Dependent Clause: "When the Trojans dragged him out".
(This is incomplete on its own and depends on the rest of the sentence to make sense.)
