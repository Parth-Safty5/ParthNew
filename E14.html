Q.1) Read the poem and complete the following activities

A1. Simple Factual Activities

(1) Say WHO

(a) Reminds the poet of his childhood
→ The butterfly

(b) Is afraid to touch the butterfly
→ The poet s sister

(c) Is like a hunter
→ The Poet

(d) Is the poet s sister
→ Emmeline

 

(2) Is the poet a grown-up person or a child?

Ans. The poet is a grown-up person.

 

A2. Complex Factual Activities

(1) Pick out four expressions from the first stanza, where the poet pleads with the butterfly not to go away:

  • Stay near me
  • Do not take thy flight
  • A little longer stay in sight
  • Do not yet depart

A3. Poetic Devices

(1) Write the rhyming pairs from the poem:

  • springs wings
  • I butterfly
  • rough bough bush
  • days - plays

 

(2) Find any two examples of inversion from the poem and rewrite them using regular word order:

  • A little longer stay in sight! → Stay a little longer in sight.
  • Much converse do I find in thee. → I do find much converse in thee
