1.1 Walk a little slower...
Title: Walk a little slower...
Poet: Anonymous (Unknown)
Rhyme Scheme: The rhyme scheme of the poem is 'abcb'.
Figures of Speech: Metaphor, Alliteration, Repetition.
Central Idea / Theme: The poem is a plea (request) by a small child to his father. The child wants to observe and imitate his fatherâ€™s actions and way of life. He wants his father to guide him properly so that when the child grows up, he can guide his own child in the right way. It highlights the importance of a father as a role model.
1.3 'Hope' is the thing with feathers
Title: 'Hope' is the thing with feathers
Poet: Emily Dickinson
Rhyme Scheme: Stanza 1 & 2: 'abab', Stanza 3: 'abcb'.
Figures of Speech: Metaphor, Personification, Alliteration.
Central Idea / Theme: This poem describes 'Hope' as a bird that lives in the human soul. Hope is ever-lasting and it sings even in the most difficult times (storms). It tells us that hope is a selfless quality that helps us survive even when things are very hard.
2.1 Comparisons
Title: Comparisons
Poet: Anonymous
Rhyme Scheme: The rhyme scheme is 'abcb'.
Figures of Speech: Simile, Alliteration, Antithesis.
Central Idea / Theme: The poem celebrates the diversity in the world. It compares different things like size, speed, temperature, and shape. The central idea is that everything in this world is unique, and its beauty lies in its comparison with others.
2.4 Please Listen!
Title: Please Listen!
Poet: Anonymous
Rhyme Scheme: Free Verse (No regular rhyme scheme).
Figures of Speech: Repetition, Alliteration, Simile.
Central Idea / Theme: The poem expresses the feelings of a teenager who wants to be heard. The speaker asks elders to just listen instead of giving advice. It shows that people sometimes just need to be heard silently to find their own solutions.
3.1 Coromandel Fishers
Title: Coromandel Fishers
Poet: Sarojini Naidu
Rhyme Scheme: The rhyme scheme is 'aabb'.
Figures of Speech: Metaphor, Personification, Simile.
Central Idea / Theme: The poem is a call to the fishermen to start their work at dawn. It describes the deep bond between the fishermen and nature, reflecting their courage and hard work.
3.3 To a Butterfly
Title: To a Butterfly
Poet: William Wordsworth
Rhyme Scheme: Stanza 1: 'aabbcddcc', Stanza 2: 'aabbcdeec'.
Figures of Speech: Apostrophe, Personification, Inversion.
Central Idea / Theme: The poet is fascinated by the beauty of a butterfly. It reminds him of his happy childhood days and expresses his desire to stay connected with nature's simple joys.

4.3 The Silver House
Title: The Silver House
Poet: John Lea
Rhyme Scheme: The rhyme scheme is 'abab'.
Figures of Speech: Metaphor, Alliteration, Personification.
Central Idea / Theme: A fantasy about the Moon, described as a 'Silver House'. It poetically explains the phases of the moon and the "man in the moon" who lives inside it.
