Ans: The narrator explains that if you wish to speak even a single word, that word must first pass through two "fences." The first fence is our lips; before any word is spoken, it must pass through this barrier. The second fence is the rows of our teeth. This setup reminds us to be careful and think twice before letting any words escape.

A5. Personal Response

(1) Was Disraeli trying to give a scientific reason? Was he only trying to give a message in a light hearted but effective way?

Ans: Disraeli was not trying to give a scientific reason about why man has two ears and one mouth. Instead, he was offering a meaningful message for mankind. He suggested that if humans had two mouths and only one ear, we would talk twice as much and listen half as much, which would fundamentally change our nature and appearance.

(2) Write the biological reasons why man's ears and mouth have developed the way they are now.

Ans: Ears are sensory organs designed to interpret sound. Having two ears allows for better "binaural hearing," which helps in capturing sounds from all directions and locating their source. The mouth is primarily developed for feeding and speaking. In the biological evolution of animals, a single mouth is more efficient for eating, while dual ears are essential for gathering all surrounding sound signals for safety and communication.
