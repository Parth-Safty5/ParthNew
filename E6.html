2.1 Comparisons

Warming up!
Chit-Chat
1. What is your favourite colour? What does it remind you of?
Answer: My favourite colour is blue. It reminds me of the clear sky and the deep ocean.

2. Are you afraid of the dark? What does it make you think of?
Answer: No, I am not afraid of the dark. It makes me think of starry nights and peaceful sleep.

3. If you were asked to design a big garden, what will you include in it?
Answer: I will include a fountain, a variety of colorful flowers, a small pond, and comfortable benches.

6. Given below are some idiomatic comparisons with ‘like’. Can you guess their meaning? Look them up in a good dictionary. You won’t find them under ‘like’. Which words will you look up to find these comparisons?
Like a bull in a china shop: (Look up: Bull)
Meaning: Someone who is very clumsy or careless in a delicate situation.

Like a cat on a hot tin roof: (Look up: Cat)
Meaning: Someone who is very nervous, restless, or anxious.

Like a red rag to a bull: (Look up: Rag)
Meaning: Something that is certain to make someone very angry.

Like a cat that stole the cream: (Look up: Cat)
Meaning: Looking very pleased with oneself or self-satisfied.

Like water off a duck’s back: (Look up: Water)
Meaning: Criticisms or warnings that have no effect on the person they are directed at.

Memory like a sieve: (Look up: Memory or Sieve)
Meaning: Someone who forgets things very easily.
