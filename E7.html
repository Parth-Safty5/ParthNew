1. Discuss the challenges that people have to face due to disabilities.
Disability Challenges Necessary facilities
Hearing loss and deafness Inability to hear announcements, alarms, or conversations. Sign language interpreters, visual alarms (flashing lights), closed captioning on screens.
Vision loss and blindness Difficulty navigating environments and reading written information. Braille signage, tactile paving on sidewalks, audio signals at traffic lights, ramps.
Speech disorders Difficulty communicating needs and interacting socially. Augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) devices, patient support services.
Physical disability Limited mobility, difficulty accessing buildings or transportation. Ramps, elevators, wider doorways, accessible toilets, dedicated parking spaces.
Intellectual disability Challenges with communication, learning, and self-care. Simplified signage, sensory-friendly spaces, supportive staff training.
Learning disorder Difficulty processing information, reading, or writing. Assistive technology (screen readers), quiet study areas, specialized teaching aids.

5. Write about your own experience
Outline:

Introduction: Learning to ride a bicycle.
Body: The fear of falling, the feeling of balance, and the moment my father let go.
Conclusion: The joy of independence and success.

Composition:

I still remember the first time I successfully rode a bicycle without training wheels. I was eight years old and terrified of falling. My father took me to a local park to practice. Initially, I was very shaky and relied heavily on him to hold the seat. However, I kept pedaling harder, trying to find my balance. After many failed attempts, I finally managed to keep the bicycle straight for a few seconds. Suddenly, I realized my father had let go, and I was riding on my own! I felt a rush of adrenaline and immense joy. That moment taught me that persistence leads to success.
6. Gather more information about the following:
(a) Different types of impairment:
Visual Impairment: Blindness or low vision.
Hearing Impairment: Deafness or hard of hearing.
Motor Impairment: Physical difficulties affecting movement.
Cognitive/Learning Impairment: Conditions like dyslexia or intellectual disabilities.

(b) How modern technology can be used to overcome these problems:
Screen readers and braille displays for visual impairment.
Hearing aids and closed captioning for hearing loss.
Wheelchairs and voice-activated control systems for motor impairments.
Educational apps and speech-to-text software for learning disorders.
