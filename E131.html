II.                Creative Writing: Building a Narrative

Context: Ye sentences ek group activity ke liye design kiye gaye hain jo ek interesting story banate hain.

  1. The Hero: Once there was a brave young explorer who loved visiting forgotten places.
  2. The Setting: Who lived in a quiet, peaceful village at the base of a huge, misty mountain.
  3. The Diet: He/She ate only wild berries and honey collected during long morning walks.
  4. The Journey: She/He went deep into a hidden cave one day, following a strange golden light.
  5. The Discovery: There She/He saw an ancient, glowing shield that reportedly belonged to a legendary king.
  6. The Reaction: She/He was amazed by the detailed carvings on the shield that seemed to predict the future.
  7. The Decision: That is why they decided to become a Guardian of the Mountains , protecting ancient secrets forever.

2. Fact-Check: Correcting Sentences

(a) Incorrect: Troy traded in cattle and grass.

Correct: Troy was a wealthy city trading in grain, meat, and wool.

(b) Incorrect: Trojans jumped over gates to fight.

Correct: The Trojans would come out of their large gates to fight on the open plains.

(c) Incorrect: Helen escaped with Menelaus.

Correct: Helen was actually taken to Troy by Paris, a Trojan prince.

(d) Incorrect: Greeks always defeated Trojans.

Correct: Both sides were equally matched, and victories shifted back and forth.

(e) Incorrect: Both enemies were eager to fight.

Correct: Both sides were actually tired of the war and missed their homes.

(f) Incorrect: Troy was attacked only for its wealth.

Correct: The Greeks attacked primarily to avenge the kidnapping of Helen.

 

3. Action and Response (Counter-Action)

(a) Action: Helen eloped with Paris.

Response: The Greeks sailed to Troy to take revenge and bring her back.

(b) Action: Greeks attack Troy.

Response: Trojans defended from behind their walls, and the war lasted ten years.

(c) Action: Hector killed by Achilles.

Response: King Priam begged for his son’s body, and later Achilles was killed by a poisoned arrow.

(d) Action: Siege lasts ten years.

Response: Greeks used a clever plan involving a massive wooden horse.

4. Epic Comparison: The Kurukshetra War

This answer provides a brief overview of the war described in the Mahabharata.

  • Cause: A throne dispute in Hastinapur between the Pandavas and Kauravas .
  • The Armies: The Pandava army (7 units) faced the larger Kaurava army (11 units).
  • Heroes: Key figures included Lord Krishna, Arjuna, and Bhishma .
  • Duration: The battle lasted for 18 days.
  • Outcome: The Pandavas won, but the war caused massive destruction .
