Q.1 Read the following passage and do the activities:

A1. Simple Factual Activity:

Correct the following sentences using facts from the passage:

(a) Troy traded in cattle and grass with other cities.
Corrected: Actually, Troy was known for trading goods and grain with other cities.

(b) During war, Trojans jumped out of the fort and their soldiers to fight the enemy.
Corrected: In times of war, the Trojans would come out through their city gates to confront the enemy.

(c) Helen escaped with Menelaus.
Corrected: Helen didn t escape with Menelaus she eloped with Paris.

(d) Troy was attacked because it was a strong, rich city.
Corrected: The real reason Troy was attacked was because the Prince of Troy convinced Helen, the wife of a Greek king, to go with him. The Greeks wanted revenge.

 

A2. Complex Factual Activities:

(1) State the counteraction for the following actions:

(a) Helen eloped with Paris.
Counteraction: In response, the Greeks sailed to Troy and began a long siege to take revenge.

(b) The Greeks sailed to Troy and attacked it.
Correction: The Trojans put up a strong fight, and the siege lasted for ten long years.

(2) How did the location of Troy help it to grow into a very rich/prosperous city?
Answer: Troy s location was perfect for trade and farming. It was close to the sea, and its valleys were fertile enough to grow plenty of corn.

(3) How were the Trojans protected?
Answer: During wartime, the Trojans were shielded by the city s strong walls and defended by courageous soldiers.

(4) What was the cause of the ten-year war between the Greeks and the Trojans?
Answer: The war began because Paris, the Prince of Troy, convinced Helen wife of the Greek king Menelaus to run away with him. This deeply angered the Greeks.

Q.2 Read the following passage and do the activities:

A1. Simple Factual Activity

Correct the following statements using facts from the passage:

(a) The Greek armies and heroes always defeated the Trojans.
Corrected: Sometimes the Trojans had the upper hand, and sometimes the Greeks did.

(b) Both the enemies were eager to continue fighting.
Corrected: The Trojans were tired of being trapped inside their city, while the Greeks were longing to return home.

 

A2. Complex Factual Activity

(1) State the counteraction for the following action:

(a) Hector was killed by Achilles.
Counteraction: Later, Achilles himself was killed by a poisoned arrow that struck his heel.

(b) The siege continued for ten long years.
Counteraction: Even after all that time, the fighting didn t stop. The Trojans were exhausted from being trapped inside their city.

(c) The fighting went on daily.
Counteraction: Yet the siege never ended. The Greeks couldn t capture the city, and the Trojans couldn t drive them away back to Greece.

 

(2) Who were the brave heroes on either side who were killed in the war? How?
Answer: Many great warriors lost their lives during the war. Among them were Hector, the Trojan hero who died defending his city, and Achilles, the Greek hero who also fell in battle.

Writing Skill

Write about a battle or war from Indian epics. Include its cause, enemy armies, heroes, duration, and final outcome.

Answer:
One of the most famous wars in Indian mythology is the battle of Kurukshetra from the Mahabharata. The conflict began when the Kauravas forcefully took control of the kingdom of Hastinapur, which rightfully belonged to the Pandavas. Despite repeated requests, the Kauravas refused to return the kingdom, leading to war.

The Kaurava army included 100 brothers, Shakuni, and Karna, while the Pandavas were supported by King Drupad, King Virat, and others. Legendary warriors like Arjun, Bhim, Nakul, Sahdev, and Lord Krishna fought on the Pandava side, while Duryodhan, Duhshasan, Karn, and Bhishma stood with the Kauravas.

The war lasted for 18 days and ended with the victory of the Pandavas. Ashwathama was defeated, and the Pandavas regained control of Hastinapur.
