3.2 The Fall of Troy

Warming up!
Chit-Chat
• Do you know stories from any epics or mythological poems?
Answer: Yes, I am familiar with the Ramayana and Mahabharata from India, as well as the Iliad and the Odyssey from Greek mythology.

• Who is your favourite mythological character? What do you like about that character?
Answer: My favourite character is Arjuna from the Mahabharata. I admire his incredible focus, his dedication to mastering archery, and how he sought wisdom from Lord Krishna during difficult times.

• Who is your favourite warrior?
Answer: My favourite warrior is Achilles. He was known for his unmatched strength and bravery in the Trojan War, though he had a famous weakness (his heel).

• If you could visit a place in the ancient world, which place would you choose? Which event would you like to see?
Answer: I would choose to visit the Ancient City of Troy. I would love to see the event of the "Trojan Horse" to witness how the Greeks used strategy and wit to enter the city walls.
Building a Story
The goal of this activity is to see what happens when different people contribute to a story blindly. If you are doing this solo, here is a cohesive, fun example of how those blanks could be filled:
• Once there was a brave young knight named Leo.
• Who lived in a cloud-topped castle made of sparkling glass.
• He/She ate golden apples that gave him the ability to speak to animals.
• She/He went to the Dark Forest to find a lost dragon egg.
• There She/He saw a giant owl wearing a silver crown.
• She/He was surprised to find that the owl was actually a guardian.
• That is why he returned home with a new friend instead of a dragon egg.
English Workshop (Part 1)
1. Find antonyms of the following from the passage.
(a) barren-fertile
(b) offended-pleased / honored
(c) cowardly-bravely / heroic
(d) peace-war
(e) surrender-resist / fight
(f) exposed-protected / hidden
(g) defenceless-safe / fortified
2. Correct the following sentences using facts from the passage.
(a) Troy traded in cattle and grass with other cities.
Corrected: Troy traded in goods and grain with other cities.

(b) During war, Trojans jumped over the fort gates and their soldiers to fight the enemy.
Corrected: During war, Trojans came out of the fort gates to fight the enemy, or fought from the top of the high walls.

(c) Helen escaped with Menelaus.
Corrected: Helen escaped with Paris, a prince of Troy (Menelaus was her husband whom she left).

(d) The Greek armies and heroes always defeated the Trojans.
Corrected: The Greek armies and heroes could not defeat the Trojans for ten long years; the Trojans fought back bravely.

(e) Both the enemies were eager to continue fighting.
Corrected: Both the enemies were weary (tired) of the long war and longed for peace.

(f) Troy was attacked because it was a strong, rich city.
Corrected: Troy was attacked because Paris had carried off Helen, the wife of the Greek King Menelaus.
3. State the counter action
(a) Helen eloped with Paris: (1) The Greeks sailed to Troy to take revenge and bring her back.

(b) The Greeks sailed to Troy and attacked it: (2) The Trojans came out of their gates and fought the Greeks, or defended themselves from the walls.

(c) Hector was killed by Achilles: (3) The Trojans continued to resist, though they were heartbroken by the loss of their great leader.

(d) The siege continued for ten long years: (4) Both sides grew weary of the war, and the Greeks eventually turned to a clever trick (the Wooden Horse).

(e) The fighting went on daily: (5) Many heroes on both sides were killed, but the city of Troy remained untaken.
4. Indian Epic Comparison: The Kurukshetra War
Cause: The dispute over the kingdom of Hastinapura between the Pandavas and Kauravas.

The enemy armies: The Pandavas (7 Akshauhinis) vs. the Kauravas (11 Akshauhinis).

Heroes: Arjuna, Bhishma, Karna, Krishna, and Bhima.

Duration: 18 days.

Final outcome: The Pandavas won, but with massive loss of life on both sides.
