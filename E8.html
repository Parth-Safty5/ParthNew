Chit-Chat
• Who is your favourite comedian?
Answer: My favourite comedian is Charlie Chaplin because he could make people laugh without saying a single word.

• What do you like better – to listen to a joke, or to tell a joke?
Answer: I like to listen to jokes better because I enjoy the surprise of a good punchline, though telling them is fun when I want to make my friends happy.

• Do you like cartoons and cartoon films? Which one do you like best?
Answer: Yes, I love cartoons. My favourite one is Tom and Jerry because their endless chase is always hilarious.

ENGLISH WORKSHOP
1. Answer the following questions:
(a) Did Twain tell the barber who he was?
Answer: No, Twain did not tell the barber who he was; he maintained his anonymity during the conversation.

(b) Was Twain particular about how he dressed when he was visiting friends?
Answer: No, Twain was known for being somewhat casual or indifferent about his dress, often valuing comfort over strict social formality.

(c) Was Twain particular about what words he used?
Answer: Yes, as a professional writer and orator, Twain was extremely particular about choosing "the right word" rather than "the almost right word."

(d) Do you think Twain approved of spelling reforms? Give reasons for your answer.
Answer: Yes, Twain was a supporter of simplified spelling. He felt that English spelling was unnecessarily complex and illogical, and he often joked about its inconsistencies.

(e) How did Twain explain the spider in the paper? What did he want to highlight?
Answer: Twain told an editor that a spider in a newspaper was merely looking to see which merchants were not advertising, so it could spin a web over their doors in peace. He wanted to highlight the necessity of advertising for business success.
2. Read the following and write the two meanings of ‘mind’ and ‘matter’:
‘Age is an issue of mind over matter. If you don’t mind, it doesn’t matter.’
Mind
= The intellect, willpower, or consciousness.
= To be bothered by or to care about something.

Matter
= Physical substance/the body.
= To be of importance or significance.

Language Study
7. Passive Voice
In the passive voice, the object of the action becomes the subject of the sentence. The focus shifts from who did the action to what was affected.

Examples of Passive Construction (be + past participle):
Active: Sonali ate the mango.
Passive: The mango was eaten.
With the 'doer': This pen was given to me by my Aunt.

Note: Only transitive verbs (verbs that have an object) can be changed into the passive voice.
8. Refer to the Language Study pages and read the entries 'irony' and 'pun'. Find one example of each from the passage or the quotations.
Pun: The quote in Question 2 is a perfect example of a pun, as it plays on the double meanings of the words "mind" and "matter."

Irony: An example of irony in Twain's life is when he visited a town to give a lecture and found the local barber recommending against going to see "Mark Twain" because he wasn't very good.
