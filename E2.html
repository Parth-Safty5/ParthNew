How often should the teacher give you a test?
I think a test once a month is good so that we have enough time to learn and understand the lessons before being graded.

Summary of "1.2 The Fun They Had."

Summary of "1.2 The Fun They Had."

Setting the Scene

The story is set in the future, on May 17, 2157. In this world, children do not go to school buildings or have human teachers. Instead, they study at home using mechanical teachers (large robots with screens).


The Discovery

A 13-year-old boy named Tommy finds a "real" book in the attic of his house. This is a big deal because, in 2157, all books are digital "telebooks" that move on a screen.

His friend, 11-year-old Margie, is fascinated. The pages of the book are yellow and crinkly. To them, it is strange that the words stay still on the paper instead of moving like they do on a computer.


The Difference in Schools

Tommy explains that the book is about school. Margie hates school, mainly because her mechanical teacher has been giving her difficult geography tests that she keeps failing. She can't imagine why anyone would want to write a book about it.

Tommy tells her that schools "centuries ago" were very different:

The Teachers: They were human beings, not robots.

The Building: All the kids went to a special building instead of studying in a room next to their bedroom.

The Learning: Kids of the same age learned the same things together and could help each other with homework.

Margie’s Realization

Margie’s mother calls her to start her lessons. As Margie sits in front of her mechanical teacher—which is currently teaching her how to add fractions—she can't stop thinking about the "old days."

She imagines the laughter and shouting of children in the schoolyard. She thinks about how much fun it must have been to study with friends and have a human teacher. The story ends with Margie dreaming about "the fun they had."

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Why did Margie hate school?

Margie hated school because it was mechanical and lonely. She had to sit alone in a room next to her bedroom every day (except Saturday and Sunday). Her mechanical teacher was giving her difficult geography tests one after another, and she was performing poorly, which made her feel frustrated.

2. What is a "Telebook"?

A telebook is a digital book that can be read on a screen, like a television or computer. Unlike paper books, the words in a telebook move across the screen, and one screen can store millions of different books.

3. Who was the County Inspector and what did he do?

The County Inspector was a "round little man with a red face" who repaired mechanical teachers. He came to Margie's house because her teacher was teaching geography too fast. He slowed down the geography sector to an average ten-year-old level so Margie could understand it better.

4. What were the "strange" things about the real book Tommy found?

To Margie and Tommy, the real book was strange because:

• The words stood still instead of moving.

• The pages were yellow and crinkled.

• When they turned back to a page they had already read, it had the same words on it.

6. Why did Margie think the kids in the old days had "fun"?

Margie thought they had fun because they weren't alone. They went to a common building, played in the schoolyard, and could talk to each other about their homework and help one another. To her, a school full of people sounded much more exciting than a cold, silent machine.
